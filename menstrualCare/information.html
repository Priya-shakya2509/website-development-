What is Menstruation?

Menstruation (or a period) is a natural process where the body sheds the lining of the uterus. This usually happens once a month and is part of the menstrual cycle, which helps the body prepare for pregnancy.

When Does It Start?

Most girls start their periods between the ages of 10 and 15. It can be earlier or later depending on your body.

Common Symptoms

  • Stomach cramps
  • Back pain
  • Mood swings
  • Headaches
  • Bloating

Frequently Asked Questions

What are sanitary pads?

Sanitary pads are absorbent products worn inside underwear to soak up menstrual blood.

Can I bathe during my period?

Yes! In fact, warm water helps reduce cramps and keeps you clean.

Is it normal to feel emotional?

Yes, hormonal changes can affect mood. Try resting and talking to someone you trust.

Myth vs Fact

Myth

You should not exercise during your period.

Fact

Light exercise like yoga can reduce cramps and lift your mood.

Myth

You can't take a bath on your period.

Fact

Bathing is not only safe, it helps relieve period pain.
